For first-time readers

The case in one page.

This is not primarily a claim that the diagnosis was wrong. It is a claim that the institution exercised finality before it had completed the inquiry required to justify finality.

The governing rule

Finality must follow inquiry. It must not precede it.

A system may suspect, treat provisionally, watch, test, defer medication, seek specialist input, or preserve uncertainty. It may not convert unresolved inquiry into durable administrative fact while the material questions remain open.

The ten-point version

  1. The case entered through a foot-centred gait failure, not through a clean abstract Parkinson's question.
  2. The GP referral preserved diagnostic openness while making the feet part of the accepted problem.
  3. The discharge record preserved red flags: youth, atypicality, dystonic-looking gait, genetic possibility, unresolved feet, and deferred dopaminergic therapy.
  4. Levodopa was not merely treatment in this setting. It was also a discriminator capable of clarifying the case.
  5. If levodopa was withheld for caution, certainty had to be withheld too.
  6. The clinician's non-movement-disorder disclaimer should have produced referral, supervision, or preserved provisionality.
  7. The LTI certification turned a provisional hypothesis into portable administrative reliance.
  8. The DAT scan supported part of the frame, but it arrived after closure and did not answer the feet.
  9. The patient's protest did not need technical language. It was the unresolved referral problem speaking from the bed.
  10. Later records kept returning to the same foot-centred contradiction, proving that the problem was managed, not resolved.
If no one could know, no one could certify as if they knew.

The cleanest formulation

The institution did not merely reach a doubtful conclusion. It spoke and acted with certainty after it had made knowing unsafe, then organised its records and governance to preserve that certainty over time.
