Top 10 / Artefact hall

Artefacts in the Hall of Foreclosure.

The case is not built from allegation inward. It is built from artefacts outward. Each document either defines the problem, closes over it, hardens it, or later exposes the cost of closure.

  1. The GP referral.
    The entry artefact. It defines the accepted problem: gait, neurological signs, function, and the feet.
  2. The first hospital problem representation.
    The translation point where the body begins becoming a diagnostic abstraction.
  3. The discharge summary.
    The closure artefact: rich with warning signs, yet administratively final.
  4. The levodopa-deferral language.
    The hinge artefact: caution around treatment becomes dangerous when certainty is not equally restrained.
  5. The non-specialist disclaimer.
    The jurisdiction artefact: a limit is recorded without pathway consequence.
  6. The LTI certification.
    The statutory artefact: suspicion becomes reliance.
  7. The DAT scan addendum.
    The post-closure artefact: confirmation after the frame has already landed.
  8. The bedside protest.
    The missing artefact: the patient reasserts the referral problem before discharge finality.
  9. The longitudinal neurology notes.
    The return artefact: the feet keep coming back into the record.
  10. The genetic confirmation letter.
    The completion artefact: later causation illuminates why the earlier warnings mattered.
The document that was supposed to close the case instead discloses why the case was not closed.
