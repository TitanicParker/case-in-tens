Top 10 Times the Feet Re-entered Neurology

The feet were never safely outside the case. They kept returning as the unresolved signal.

Top 10 Questions the Record Cannot Answer

  1. If final, why uncertain?
  2. If uncertain, why certified?
  3. If separate, why responsive?
  4. If responsive, why peripheral?
  5. If Parkinsonian, why podiatry?
  6. If podiatric, why dopaminergic response?
  7. If open, why closed?
  8. If closed, why still returning to the feet?
  9. If reviewed, why not revised?
  10. If no one could know, why certify as if they knew?

Top 10 Things the Record Made Non-Governing

  1. The foot-centred entry problem.
  2. The dystonic-looking gait.
  3. The footdrop-like movement that was expressly not foot drop.
  4. The patient's youth.
  5. The atypical presentation.
  6. The possible genetic disease.
  7. The deferred levodopa discriminator.
  8. The possibility of future regret.
  9. The non-movement-disorder disclaimer.
  10. The bedside protest.

Top 10 Impossible Institutional Positions

  1. Open for defence, closed for certification.
  2. Complex enough to excuse uncertainty, settled enough to certify.
  3. Feet central enough to document, peripheral enough to export.
  4. Levodopa important enough to defer carefully, irrelevant enough not to affect finality.
  5. DAT scan decisive after the diagnosis was already relied upon.
  6. Patient cognitively capable, but not given the diagnostic architecture.
  7. Pain not Parkinsonian, yet relieved by Parkinson's medication.
  8. Review continuing, but the premise never vulnerable.
  9. Disclaimer recorded, but no escalation produced.
  10. Documents nuanced, but certainty unqualified.

Top 10 Blades

  1. Reasonableness of suspicion is not reasonableness of closure.
  2. If levodopa was withheld, certainty had to be withheld too.
  3. A symptom can be discussed forever without being allowed to govern.
  4. The omission made the record cleaner than the event.
  5. Support is not sequence.
  6. Recognition without consequence.
  7. The record wandered, but it did not migrate.
  8. Review of a patient is not review of a premise.
  9. A contradiction repeatedly managed is not a contradiction resolved.
  10. The record is not a defence exhibit. It is the problem.
