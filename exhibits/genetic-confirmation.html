The Genetic Confirmation

The later causal clarification that illuminates why earlier uncertainty mattered.

GeneticsCausationCompletion

What it is

The genetic confirmation is a later document that gives causal structure to features that had been visible in the earlier record: young onset, atypicality, dystonic features, preserved cognition, and the foot-centred pattern.

Why it matters

It does not create the original ordering defect. The defect is visible earlier. But it shows that the earlier warnings were not decorative or theoretical. They were signs pointing toward a different governing account.

What it shows

It completes the evidential arc. The question is not whether the hospital should have known the final genetic answer at the beginning. The question is why it acted with finality while the signs that should have prevented finality were already in the record.

Later confirmation does not create the warning. It reveals what the warning was warning about.
