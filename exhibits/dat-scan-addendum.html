The DAT Scan Addendum

Supportive evidence that arrived after the diagnostic and administrative frame had already moved.

ScanSequenceConfirmation

What it is

The scan addendum is supportive clinical evidence. It may reinforce part of the Parkinsonian frame. But it must be read in time, not in isolation.

Why it matters

The scan cannot be treated as though it governed closure if closure had already been performed. Evidence after the event can reassure. It cannot retroactively make earlier finality properly sequenced.

What it shows

It shows confirmation after expectation. It may support a dopaminergic deficit, but it does not answer the unresolved foot-centred problem, the deferred discriminator, the protest, or the specialist-ownership issue.

Support is not sequence.
