The Neurology Notes

The longitudinal record that repeatedly returns to the same foot-centred problem.

LongitudinalFeetReturn

What it is

The neurology notes are the afterlife of the original closure. They show how the same unresolved signal continued to appear across later reviews, medication discussions, function, pain, gait, and patient concern.

Why it matters

The later record does not simply show continuing care. It shows continuing contradiction. The foot problem is repeatedly discussed, repeatedly linked to function and medication timing, and repeatedly translated back into the governing frame.

What it shows

It shows that review of a patient is not the same as review of a premise. The patient can be followed for years while the original closure remains structurally protected.

The record did not forget the feet. It kept rediscovering them, then refusing to let them govern.
