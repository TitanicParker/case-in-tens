The Protest and the Omission

The moment when the patient restored the original problem before closure, and the closing record did not preserve that challenge.

ProtestOmissionRecord integrity

What it is

This exhibit concerns the bedside objection that the foot problem had not been addressed before discharge, and the absence of that objection from the discharge artefact.

Why it matters

The protest was not a later theory. It was a real-time challenge to discharge completeness. Because the issue concerned the same foot-centred problem that brought the case into hospital, it was material to the safety and integrity of the closing record.

What it shows

It shows the difference between a clean record and a complete record. A discharge summary containing the protest would force the reader to ask whether closure answered the patient's challenge or merely moved around it.

The omission made the record cleaner than the event.
