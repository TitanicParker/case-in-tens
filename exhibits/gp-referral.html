The GP Referral

The document that defines the problem the hospital accepted.

EntryFeetScope

What it is

The referral is the entry document. It frames the patient as presenting with neurological signs, functional deterioration, gait difficulty, and foot-centred symptoms. It does not close the diagnostic question. It hands the question to neurology.

Why it matters

The referral fixes the accepted scope of inquiry. The hospital did not merely receive a question about a label. It received a problem about walking, function, and the feet.

That means later finality had to answer the relationship between the neurological signs and the foot-centred presentation.

What it shows

Diagnostic openness at entry. The referral preserves the possibility of Parkinsonism or another neurological disorder. That openness becomes the baseline against which the discharge and certification must be judged.

The case entered through the feet.
