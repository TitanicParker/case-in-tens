Teaching terms

Glossary of the hall.

The site should teach its own language. Each term is a tool for reading the record in sequence.

Administrative foreclosure

The conferral of administrative, statutory, or institutional finality before the diagnostic sequence has completed.

Finality

A reliance-creating act: diagnosis line, certification, closure, pathway, or record state that future actors are invited to treat as settled.

Inquiry

The unresolved process of testing, explaining, falsifying, or preserving uncertainty before finality is exercised.

Principal discriminator

A test or intervention capable of materially clarifying the diagnostic frame. In this archive, levodopa is treated as the central example.

Non-governing fact

A fact that is recorded but denied power over the conclusion.

Red flag neutralisation

The process by which warning signs are documented but converted into background detail.

Explanatory capture

A closed frame's tendency to absorb contradictory facts rather than become vulnerable to them.

Manufactured ignorance

A condition in which a cognitively capable patient is not given the architecture needed to object meaningfully.

Support is not sequence

Evidence may support a suspicion without proving that earlier finality was properly ordered.

Review of patient vs review of premise

Follow-up can continue inside a closed diagnosis without the diagnosis itself becoming vulnerable to displacement.
