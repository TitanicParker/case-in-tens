Top 10 / The signal

Times the feet re-entered neurology.

The feet were never safely outside the case. They were the entry signal, the protest signal, the medication-response signal, and the longitudinal return of the unresolved problem.

  1. The referral entered through gait and feet.
    The accepted problem was not an abstract diagnosis. It was a foot-centred failure of walking with neurological signs.
  2. The discharge summary preserved the feet but did not obey them.
    Corns, calluses, pain, gait, and dystonic suggestion were recorded, then made non-governing.
  3. The footdrop-like movement was not foot drop.
    If power was normal, the phenomenon required central movement-disorder reasoning.
  4. The patient protested before discharge.
    He did not introduce a new fact. He restored the original fact.
  5. Podiatry did not answer sequence.
    Surface care was not a neurological explanation.
  6. Procyclidine withdrawal brought back the outside-left-foot phenomenon.
    A centrally acting medication changed the same focal site.
  7. The 2020 note called the foot throw parkinsonian.
    The record itself pulled the feet back into neurology.
  8. The 2023 death-wish referral centred on bilateral burning foot pain.
    The original signal returned as crisis.
  9. The record acknowledged misalignment about the feet.
    The patient's concern that much related to the feet was described as reasonable and justifiable.
  10. Sinemet relief kept the feet dopaminergically material.
    The symptom repeatedly treated as difficult to classify kept responding inside the Parkinson-medication field.
The record did not forget the feet. It kept rediscovering them, then refusing to let them govern.
