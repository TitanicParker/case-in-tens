Evidence hall

The artefacts.

Each exhibit should eventually link to its primary record, transcript, and related Top 10s. The design principle is museum clarity: what it is, why it matters, what it shows.

Entry artefact

The GP Referral

Why it matters: it defines the accepted problem: gait, function, neurological signs, and the feet.

What it shows: diagnostic openness at entry.

Closure artefact

The Discharge Summary

Why it matters: it records incompleteness while performing completion.

What it shows: uncertainty preserved as prose, certainty exercised as power.

Reliance artefact

The LTI Certification

Why it matters: certification turns suspicion into portable administrative reliance.

What it shows: statutory hardening before inquiry had finished.

Post-closure artefact

The DAT Scan Addendum

Why it matters: it supports the frame after the frame had already moved.

What it shows: confirmation after closure is not inquiry before closure.

Record-integrity artefact

The Protest / Omission

Why it matters: a cognitively capable patient objected in ordinary language that the feet remained unanswered.

What it shows: the omission made the record cleaner than the event.

Longitudinal artefact

The Neurology Notes

Why it matters: the feet keep returning to the record.

What it shows: the record repeatedly rediscovered the feet, then refused to let them govern.

Governance artefact

The March 2025 Rebuttal

Why it matters: it acknowledges pain, Sinemet response, caution, and regret while preserving the frame.

What it shows: a truth can be acknowledged and denied governing force.

Causal artefact

St Vincent's Genetic Confirmation

Why it matters: it completes causal inquiry and prevents the later letter from being orphaned.

What it shows: the descriptive dystonia language becomes governing mechanism.
