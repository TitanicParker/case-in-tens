Top 10 / Defence traps

Impossible institutional positions.

These are positions the institution may want to occupy, but each one collapses into its opposite once the record is read in sequence.

  1. Open for defence, closed for certification.
    The record cannot be provisional when challenged and final when relied upon.
  2. Complex enough to excuse uncertainty, settled enough to certify.
    Complexity is the reason to preserve provisionality, not the excuse for bypassing it.
  3. Feet central enough to document, peripheral enough to export.
    A fact cannot be used to prove diligence, then denied governing force when it challenges closure.
  4. Levodopa important enough to defer carefully, irrelevant enough not to affect finality.
    If withholding mattered, the diagnostic consequences of withholding mattered too.
  5. DAT scan decisive after the diagnosis was already relied upon.
    Support after closure is not sequence before closure.
  6. Patient cognitively capable, but not given the architecture.
    Capacity matters only if the patient receives the information needed to use it.
  7. Pain not Parkinsonian, yet relieved by Parkinson's medication.
    That tension keeps the foot problem diagnostically live.
  8. Review continuing, but the premise never vulnerable.
    Follow-up is not openness unless the governing frame can actually be displaced.
  9. Disclaimer recorded, but no escalation produced.
    A disclaimer without consequence is insulation, not governance.
  10. Documents nuanced, but certainty unqualified.
    Nuance in prose cannot cure finality in act.
The defence must either say the documents meant what they said, or that the patient was governed by documents that did not mean what they said.
