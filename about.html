Publication note

What this archive is.

Case in Tens is a documentary case reader. It is designed to teach a record-based argument through short exhibit cards, Top 10 lists, essay plaques, and guided routes.

Scope

This site is interpretive. It examines the sequence, structure, and documentary logic of a case. It is not medical advice, legal advice, or an official agency publication.

The archive argues from the record outward. Its core question is not whether a final answer should have been known at the beginning. Its core question is whether finality was exercised before the record had earned it.

Evidence approach

Primary documents should be added progressively, carefully, and with redaction where appropriate. The preferred method is to begin with exhibit summaries and controlled excerpts, then link them to the relevant essays and Top 10s.

Each exhibit should state what the document is, why it matters, what it shows, and which argument it supports.

Editorial rule

The site should remain restrained. The format may be playful, but the argument should be precise. The hall-of-fame texture works because each card is short, teachable, and hard to answer.

The guiding sentence remains: make the record walk past the reader slowly, one exhibit at a time, until the contradiction becomes impossible not to see.
