Top 10 / Portable lines

Blades.

These are the sentences that travel. Each one compresses a section of the case into a line that can sit on a plaque, open an essay, or end a cross-examination sequence.

  1. Reasonableness of suspicion is not reasonableness of closure.
    The case does not need to prove suspicion was absurd. It needs to show finality was premature.
  2. If levodopa was withheld, certainty had to be withheld too.
    Caution around treatment required caution around diagnosis.
  3. A symptom can be discussed forever without being allowed to govern.
    Documentation is not the same as diagnostic authority.
  4. The omission made the record cleaner than the event.
    The protest mattered because it would have disturbed the appearance of closure.
  5. Support is not sequence.
    A later supportive test cannot retroactively make earlier finality properly ordered.
  6. Recognition without consequence.
    The red flags were not absent. They were neutralised.
  7. The record wandered, but it did not migrate.
    It explored contradictions without allowing them to move the diagnosis.
  8. Review of a patient is not review of a premise.
    Appointments can continue inside a closed frame.
  9. A contradiction repeatedly managed is not a contradiction resolved.
    Longitudinal care does not prove inquiry if the governing premise never changes.
  10. The record is not a defence exhibit. It is the problem.
    The defence has to fight its own documents.
The case is powerful because it asks the reader to believe the documents over the institution's later use of them.
