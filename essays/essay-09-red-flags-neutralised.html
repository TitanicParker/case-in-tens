Essay 9

The System Did Not Miss the Red Flags. It Neutralised Them.

On how warning signs were recorded, contained, and made non-governing.

NeutralisationTotalityRed flags
The red flags were not absent. They were made non-governing.

Thesis

The strongest version of the case is not that the system failed to notice the warning signs. It is that the warning signs were noticed, recorded, and then stripped of consequence.

That distinction matters. A missed fact is one problem. A recognised fact denied authority over the conclusion is another.

Recognition without consequence

A red flag functions as a red flag only if it changes conduct. If it is merely documented and then absorbed into a predetermined narrative, it becomes decorative. It proves not that the danger was managed, but that the danger was seen.

The recurring structure is simple: yes, this is atypical; yes, the feet are odd; yes, genetics may matter; yes, treatment is being deferred; yes, specialist ownership is limited; nevertheless, finality.

The cumulative point

Individually, each feature may be explained. Cumulatively, they make finality unsafe. The proper expert question is not whether each warning sign can be isolated and neutralised. It is what the totality required before discharge, certification, and reliance.

The answer need not be immediate certainty. The answer is preserved openness.
