Essay 7

Confirmation After Closure

On the scan addendum as post-closure reinforcement rather than governing inquiry.

DAT scanSequenceConfirmation
The scan was evidence. But in the sequence, it was evidence after closure.

Thesis

The scan may support part of the clinical frame. That does not make it the source of entitlement for acts that had already taken place. Evidence after closure is not the same as inquiry before closure.

The question is not whether the scan mattered. The question is what role it played in the order of events.

Support is not sequence

A later supportive result can reassure. It cannot travel backwards and make earlier finality properly sequenced. If the scan was part of the inquiry, finality should have waited for it. If finality did not need the scan, the scan cannot later be treated as the foundation of finality.

What it did not answer

The scan did not answer the foot-centred problem. It did not resolve the significance of dystonic-looking gait, treatment deferral, specialist limitation, patient protest, or later medication-responsive pain.

It could support suspicion. It could not erase incompleteness.
