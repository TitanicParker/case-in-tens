Essay 2

If Levodopa Was Withheld, Certainty Had to Be Withheld Too

On therapeutic caution versus diagnostic discrimination.

LevodopaDiscriminatorCaution
They did not have to give levodopa. But if they did not give levodopa, they had to leave the diagnosis open.

Thesis

The levodopa issue is powerful because it does not require the blunt argument that treatment had to begin immediately. The sharper argument is narrower: if a material discriminator was withheld, the diagnosis had to remain materially provisional.

Therapeutic caution may be legitimate. Diagnostic finality after withholding a discriminator is a different matter.

Treatment and information

In this case, levodopa was not only a medication. It was also information. Its use might have clarified the relationship between the movement disorder, gait, pain, and the feet. Its non-use therefore did not merely defer treatment. It deferred knowledge.

That is the sequencing problem. The record preserves caution around the medication but does not preserve equal caution around the conclusion.

The safe route

A safe record could have said: dopaminergic therapy is being deferred for long-term reasons, but because response may carry diagnostic significance, the diagnosis remains provisional and requires further movement-disorder review.

That would have allowed caution without pretending the diagnostic sequence had completed. The problem is not the delay alone. The problem is delay plus certainty.
