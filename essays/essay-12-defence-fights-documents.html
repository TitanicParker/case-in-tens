Essay 12

The Defence Has to Fight Its Own Documents

On institutional ambiguity, patient experience, and the problem of defending a record by contradicting it.

DefenceDocumentsAmbiguity
If the documents are ambiguous, the ambiguity was created by the institution. If the documents are not ambiguous, they support the patient.

Thesis

The defence may try to say the case was always open because review continued. But continuing appointments are not the same as continuing inquiry. A patient can be reviewed for years inside a closed frame.

The institution did not keep the case open. It kept the contradiction alive while keeping the diagnosis closed.

The record problem

The case is powerful because it is built from institutional documentation. The patient does not need to invent the contradiction. The records supply it: referral openness, discharge finality, certification reliance, later return of the feet, medication response, and explanatory instability.

That is not a patient imposing meaning on silence. It is a patient reading the institution's own words back to it.

The defence trap

If the institution says the diagnosis was final, it must explain why its own record preserved so many reasons finality was unsafe. If it says the diagnosis was open, it must explain why it certified and relied on the label as though it were final.

The record is not a defence exhibit. It is the problem.
